## Start with the signal, not with a conclusion

In RiskOps Lab, the workflow begins with an alert or a user profile that needs review. The point is not to jump straight to a final answer. The point is to understand why the signal exists and what context you need before making a judgment.

## Step 1: open the case context

When you open an alert, first identify the practical trigger:

- What type of behavior caused the alert?
- Is the concern behavioral, transactional, or profile-based?
- Does the alert already suggest a likely next step, or is it still ambiguous?

This gives you the starting frame for the review.

## Step 2: review the surrounding information

The simulator is designed to help you practice reading the case around the alert instead of staring at the alert label alone.

You should review:

- core user details
- recent activity patterns
- related alert signals
- transaction notes or placeholders
- any evidence that strengthens or weakens the original concern

## Step 3: compare signal and story

A useful beginner habit is to compare the alert signal against the customer story you currently have.

Ask:

- Does the behavior fit what this account appears to be doing?
- Is the activity still explainable after reviewing context?
- What remains unclear?

## Step 4: write a decision you can defend

The simulator is review-oriented, so writing matters. A short decision is stronger when it explains:

1. what you reviewed
2. what looked normal or unusual
3. what action makes sense next

## Step 5: use the case as a learning loop

The product is not trying to imitate live operations perfectly. It is trying to give beginners a repeatable workflow for building confidence and judgment with synthetic material.

> Good practice comes from repeated, structured review, not from pretending your first case should feel easy.

## What success looks like in the simulator

Success is not about sounding dramatic or overconfident. It is about being able to move through a case in a calm sequence:

- identify the signal
- gather the right context
- compare evidence
- write a clear next step

If you can do that consistently, the simulator is doing its job.
