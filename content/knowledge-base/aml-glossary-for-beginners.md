## Alert

An automated signal that marks activity for review. It is not a final decision.

## Escalation

Sending a case to a higher review level because the risk, uncertainty, or sensitivity is beyond normal analyst authority.

## Expected activity

The kind of account behavior that appears consistent with the customer profile or known use case.

## False positive

An alert that looks suspicious at the system level but is explainable after human review.

## Monitoring

Continuing to watch an account or activity pattern because it is notable, even if it is not yet strong enough for a higher response.

## Risk signal

A detail, pattern, or event that makes the activity worth reviewing more carefully.

## Structured review

A repeatable process for moving from signal to context to conclusion instead of jumping straight to a guess.

## Suspicious pattern

Activity that appears inconsistent, layered, unusual, or difficult to explain based on the information available.

## Transaction monitoring

System-based review of account activity to surface alerts that may need analyst investigation.

## Why this matters

Beginners do better when they connect vocabulary to workflow. A term becomes useful when you know where it shows up in an investigation and how it changes your next step.
